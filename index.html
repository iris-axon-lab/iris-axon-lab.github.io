I build things I want to exist — systems that reason over time, agents that know what they know, tools that make thinking visible. Three careers in, still driven by the same underlying question.

"How do systems know what they know — and act reliably on it?"
The thread across operations research → knowledge graphs → agentic AI
Who I am

I am energized by the intersection of rigorous system design and genuine epistemic curiosity — the places where the formal and the philosophical press against each other productively. I think in frameworks but distrust frameworks that stop being useful. I care as much about the shape of a problem as its solution.

I'm drawn to work that operates at multiple scales simultaneously — individual inference, orchestration logic, memory architecture — because the interesting bugs live in the seams between levels, not inside any single layer.

Outside the technical: classical piano, running, an ongoing project of understanding my own habitus. I'm bilingual — English and Mandarin — with deep fluency in both the Western and Chinese AI research ecosystems.

Three careers, one question
Now
Agentic AI Infrastructure
Building the runtime layer for agentic systems at scale — memory and context management, tool orchestration, planning and reasoning, long-running task lifecycle. Tens of millions of users. The hard problems are not the models. They are the memory, the orchestration, and the failure modes nobody planned for.
Before
Knowledge Graphs at Microsoft Research
Built billion-scale academic knowledge graphs adopted by the OECD and the Stanford AI Index. Learned that knowledge representation is never just a data problem — it is always also a question of what you think knowledge is.
Before that
Operations Research
PhD from USC. Learned that the best model is the one that actually gets used — and that the gap between a formally correct solution and a useful one is almost always a design problem, not a math problem.
What I think about
Memory

Memory as a first-class architectural primitive — not a bolt-on. Prospective memory as a steerability surface. The four-tier taxonomy: semantic, episodic, procedural, prospective.

Epistemic Integrity

How do long-running agentic tasks maintain reliable self-knowledge across the knowing / doing / deciding axes? What breaks when sessions end and agents restart?

Evaluation

Evaluation frameworks that don't lie to you. The difference between a metric that feels good and a grader that catches real failure modes.

Orchestration

Why orchestration is harder than it looks — tool quality, skill triggering, the compound failure rate of pipelines nobody stress-tested end to end.
